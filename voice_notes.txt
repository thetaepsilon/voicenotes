* breath control exercises from some of savvy's other student's lessons:
breathing in/out, first 5, then 7, then 12 for instance...
make sure to maintain constant flow, and _do not_ a) rely on constriction feeling over lung feeling (FALSE FOLDSSSSSS),
or b) tighten something to try to compensate for pressure loss (another trap of mine recently)

try slowing down a voiced gasp:
that's actually a breathy voiced reverse phonation and is very nearly almost always low weight (and forced low airflow).
then slide to forward and reversed phonation in breathing.
exact same pitch in and out is not necessary but try not to shoot of into a reaaaally high pitch when doing it.



apparently sometimes it makes more sense to use brown noise rather than white.
human voices have spectral rolloff like brown noise does (of varying degrees but still),
whereas white noise is equal power in all frequencies.



more on intonation hurdles:
as well as the redoing intonation inside a target pitch range as mentioned in the first question at the top,
it can sometimes help with straining and other weirdness on particular intonation patterns to do it through SOVTEs first.
this can make it easier to then retry speech in that intonation without the anomalies.
